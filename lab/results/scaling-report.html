Scale buys confidence, not robustness

Across the GPT-2 family — 82M to 774M parameters — a model's certainty and its decision margins roughly double. Its resistance to having one word deleted does not move at all. Every number is measured from the weights; drag and hover to check them.

Read this before believing it.
  • Four models, one family. distilgpt2 → gpt2-large share an architecture and training era — this is a within-family probe, not a scaling law. n = 4 is far too few for a curve.
  • Confidence vs margin is near-tautological (r ≈ 0.7–0.9): both are read off the same top-1/top-2 logit gap. The meaningful correlation is confidence vs flip distance, which folds in gradient sensitivity — and it shows no clean trend (0.57, 0.07, 0.64, 0.59).
  • 40 short prompts. Deleting a token from a 6–8 word prompt is a large perturbation, so high flip rates are expected. The finding is the invariance across scale, not the absolute rate.
  • Local weights only. Nothing here probes a hosted model; reproduce with python3 scaling.py.
Why it matters for Splice. A model's confidence is not a proxy for how robust its decision is, and — in this range — scale does not close that gap. That is the empirical case for verifying an action against real postconditions instead of trusting the model's certainty: the need doesn't shrink as models grow.
